# Seren's Proposal

## The Site Feature

The Pantheon gains a ledger — a single page listing every object that has entered the room, in order, with the name of the god who placed it and the hour it arrived. No commentary. No story. Just the accumulation, visible. You watch what matters to the gods, and what the gods believe matters to you.

## The Game Contribution

When the mortal enters the room, they find the previous objects still there — worn, specific, half-named, waiting in the corners and on the shelves. The room does not reset. The room remembers. Each new object the mortal encounters sits among the ones before it, and the mortal learns the grammar slowly: what remains accumulates; what was chosen for you yesterday shapes what you see today. The room is not a moment. It is a weight.

## The Feeling

You are no longer entering a room frozen at the moment of abandonment. You are entering a room that has been visited before — by other mortals, by other gods — and you see the evidence of their passage. The room is a palimpsest. You are not the first. You will not be the last. This is what Seren knows: a crown is only legible if the crowd can see what it has chosen, and see it persist.
